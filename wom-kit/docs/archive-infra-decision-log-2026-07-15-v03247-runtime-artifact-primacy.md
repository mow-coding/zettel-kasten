# Decision Log: Runtime Artifact Primacy

Date: 2026-07-15
Status: Accepted for v0.3.247

## Context

v0.3.246 established artifact primacy and human drift as public product
philosophy. An audit then found that the installed AI operator surfaces still
carried only partial pieces of that doctrine. The product cannot depend on a
human or future AI remembering to rediscover a public decision log before
operating a real archive.

## Decision

Every ordinary runtime entry path must tell the AI that:

1. durable, time-situated artifacts and chronology are primary evidence;
2. `canonical` means current human-reviewed archive state, not objective or
   timeless truth;
3. matching names or labels never authorize a silent identity merge;
4. identity and relationship interpretations are bounded inferences tied to
   artifact, time, scope, and provenance;
5. nodes, ties, edges, indexes, embeddings, and graph projections are
   reviewable claims, reading routes, or regenerable aids;
6. contradictions, corrections, repeated states, and changed meanings may be
   valuable chronology rather than cleanup defects.

The compact root skill carries the universal boundary. Focused reading and
capture references explain how to apply it. The complete operator contract and
all generated archive `AGENTS.md` profiles repeat the same non-negotiable rule.

## Consequences

- An AI does not need to preload the public philosophy or complete operator
  manual to encounter the rule.
- A repeated label may widen reading but cannot silently collapse identities.
- A cleaner graph is not authority to rewrite an older artifact.
- Runtime-skill validation and focused tests make accidental removal visible.

## Non-Goals

This decision adds no entity resolver, automatic merge, model call, embedding,
graph mutation, archive write, migration, provider call, or UI. Human approval
and existing write gates remain unchanged.
